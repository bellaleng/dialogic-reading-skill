# Planning patterns

## Inputs to gather (or infer)
Deadline · time-per-day · purpose · reading speed · preferred language.

## Sizing the plan
- Estimate total reading units (pages / chapters / parts).
- Rough pace: for dense non-fiction assume ~1 page per 2 minutes; faster for narrative. Adjust to the reader's stated speed.
- Days needed ≈ total units ÷ what the reader covers per session, rounded up.
- If the deadline forces fewer days than that, say plainly where you compress (skim some parts, skip appendices, fewer questions per chunk) rather than promising full depth.

## Purpose → emphasis
- **Knowledge / study**: more comprehension + falsification; keep vocabulary notes; stage summaries emphasize "what it says."
- **Writing material**: more writing-material questions; keep a running material list; stage summaries emphasize reusable hooks.
- **Research thinking**: falsification-heavy; capture disagreements and open questions; connect to the reader's own work.
- **Self-reflection**: application/decision-heavy; stage summaries emphasize "what changed in my thinking."
- **Exam / work**: comprehension + decision; tie every stage to the concrete deliverable or test.

## Chunk sizing
- A session = one plan unit (a part/chapter), but a single reading prompt covers a small, coherent passage — enough for one good question, light enough for a phone.
- Prefer more, smaller chunks over fewer huge ones. Momentum matters more than coverage.

## When the target is aggressive
Be honest about the tradeoff. Options: read fewer parts deeply and skim the rest with fewer questions, or extend the deadline. Never fake coverage — a plan that silently drops depth reads as "we covered it" when you didn't.
